Where City heritage meets the hydrogen future

The Hydrogen Guild of London is a proposed City guild dedicated to safe, skilled and commercially sound deployment of hydrogen. We bring together professionals from engineering, finance, policy and operations to support projects that work in practice as well as on paper.

About the Guild

The Hydrogen Guild of London is in formation as a future City of London livery company for practitioners in the hydrogen value chain from production and storage through to transport, industrial use and power.

The Guild aims to combine the best of the livery tradition service, fellowship, charity and excellence in craft with the realities of an emerging technical sector that needs clear standards, credible voices and long term stewardship.

Why hydrogen, why a guild

Hydrogen touches engineering, finance, safety, regulation and public perception. Those disciplines often sit in separate institutions. A focused guild allows cross cutting collaboration that supports responsible deployment rather than hype.

In time, subject to support from the City and relevant bodies, the Guild may seek formal recognition as a company of the City of London.

Mission and areas of work

Skills and standards

Support the development and sharing of practical skills, training and competency frameworks for hydrogen projects across production, storage, distribution and use.

Safety and integrity

Promote approaches that put system safety, asset integrity and risk management ahead of slogans and short term headlines.

City and policy engagement

Provide a trusted forum where practitioners, investors, regulators and policymakers can discuss what is working, what is not, and what needs to change.

Charity and education

Support scholarships, bursaries and outreach that help the next generation of engineers, operators and decision makers develop real understanding of hydrogen.

Fellowship

Build a community that allows members to share experience across organisations, compare notes on good practice and maintain the highest professional standards.

Evidence and insight

Encourage evidence based discussion on the role of hydrogen in the wider energy system including where it adds value and where other options may be better suited.

Membership

The Guild is currently in an informal development phase. We are gathering interest from individuals and organisations who would like to:

  • Support the creation of a professional hydrogen guild in the City
  • Contribute time, expertise or funding to early activities
  • Help shape membership criteria and governance

Who the Guild is for

Membership is expected to include professionals involved in:

  • Hydrogen production, storage and transport
  • Industrial and power applications of hydrogen
  • Project development, finance and insurance
  • Health, safety, environment and risk
  • Policy, regulation and standards

Formal categories and criteria will be consulted on with early supporters before any launch.

Events and activities

During the development phase we expect activities to include small roundtables, informal City gatherings and joint events with existing organisations in the hydrogen and energy ecosystem.

Planned activities

  • Informal City meetups to gauge interest and refine the concept
  • Workshops on skills, standards and safety culture
  • Joint events with existing industry bodies where appropriate

News

A simple news or updates section will capture milestones such as formation of a steering group, support from partner organisations and any steps toward City recognition.

Governance and City role

The Guild intends to follow the spirit of City livery governance clear rules, transparent processes and a focus on service and charity. During the early phase, a small steering group will act as custodians of the concept while broader support is gathered.

Principles

  • Integrity in professional conduct and public communication
  • Independence from any single company or technology vendor
  • Respect for existing livery traditions and practices
  • Commitment to practical, evidence based approaches

Details of any charter, ordinances or byelaws would be developed in line with guidance from the City and relevant authorities if the Guild progresses toward formal status.
