Where City heritage meets the hydrogen future

The Guild of Hydrogen Professionals is a proposed City guild dedicated to safe, skilled and commercially sound deployment of hydrogen. We bring together professionals from engineering, finance, policy and operations to support projects that work in practice as well as on paper.

About the Guild

The Guild of Hydrogen Professionals is in pre-formation as a future City of London livery company for practitioners across the hydrogen value chain from production and storage through to transport, conversion, industrial use and power.

The Guild aims to combine the best of the livery tradition: service, fellowship, charity and excellence in craft with the realities of a significant emerging sector that requires clear standards, credible voices and long-term stewardship.

Why hydrogen, why a guild

Hydrogen touches engineering, finance, safety, regulation and public perception. These disciplines often sit in separate institutions. A focused guild enables cross-cutting collaboration that supports responsible deployment rather than hype.

In time, subject to support from the City and relevant authorities, the Guild may seek formal recognition as a company of the City of London.

Mission and areas of work

Skills and standards

Support the development and sharing of practical skills, training and competency frameworks across the hydrogen sector.

Safety and integrity

Promote approaches that prioritise system safety, mechanical integrity and evidence-based risk management.

City and policy engagement

Provide a trusted space where practitioners, investors, regulators and policymakers can discuss what is working and what must improve.

Charity and education

Support scholarships, bursaries and outreach to develop the next generation of hydrogen professionals.

Fellowship

Build a community committed to professional excellence, mutual support and the sharing of experience.

Evidence and insight

Encourage critical, evidence-based understanding of hydrogen’s role in the wider energy and industrial system.

Governance and City role

The Guild intends to follow City livery governance principles: clarity of rules, transparency in process and a focus on service and charity.

Principles

  • Integrity in professional conduct and communication
  • Independence from any single company or technology vendor
  • Respect for livery traditions and civic responsibilities
  • Commitment to practical, evidence-based approaches

Details of any charter, ordinances or byelaws would follow City guidance if formal recognition is pursued.
